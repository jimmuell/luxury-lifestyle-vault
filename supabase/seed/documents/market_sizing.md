## 1. Summary

Luxury Lifestyle Vault is creating a category — concierge wardrobe management for affluent clients — so the market is sized top-down: the universe of affluent U.S. households times an addressable annual spend (TAM), narrowed to the Wisconsin–Arizona snowbird corridor LLV serves first (SAM), and tied to the financial model for what is realistically obtainable in the early years (SOM). All figures below are illustrative planning estimates anchored to the cited sources and the LLV financial model; validate with primary research before external use.

## 2. Method & Key Assumptions

**Affluent definition.** Roughly the top 10% of U.S. households — about $1.8M+ net worth or $210k+ income — the pool with significant discretionary and wardrobe spend.

**Revenue per member (ARPU).** Membership runs $299 (Seasonal Essentials) to $599 (Seasonal Premier) per month — about $3,600–$7,200 per year — plus on-demand fees; a blended ~$5,000 per year is used for sizing.

**Addressable share.** Not every affluent household has a large luxury wardrobe or a seasonal / multi-home lifestyle; the sizing assumes a 10–20% addressable subset.

**Category caveat.** Because this is a new category, top-down proxies are used rather than an existing market figure; treat the ranges as directional.

## 3. TAM — Total Addressable Market (U.S.)

The U.S. has roughly 23.8 million millionaires and about 12.2 million "affluent" households (top 10%), with over 6 million high-net-worth individuals holding $1M+ in investable assets. Applying a 10–20% addressable share (luxury-wardrobe-heavy or multi-home households) gives roughly 1.2–2.4 million target households. At a blended ~$5,000 per year, TAM is approximately $6–$12 billion (illustrative).

## 4. SAM — Serviceable Available Market (WI–AZ corridor)

LLV launches in the Wisconsin–Arizona snowbird corridor, centered on Scottsdale — an affluent winter-resident hub whose metro population swells by up to ~10% each season, adding tens of thousands of seasonal residents. Estimating ~50,000–150,000 affluent seasonal and resident households reachable in the early phase, at ~$5,000 per year, SAM is approximately $250–$750 million (illustrative).

## 5. SOM — Serviceable Obtainable Market (early years)

The founding-member pilot opens in October 2026. The financial model anchors the near term: about 12 members and ~$55.7K of revenue in Year 1, scaling to ~$612K by Year 3 — well under 1% of the SAM, an intentionally small, high-touch start. The growth path is to deepen penetration of the corridor and then replicate the model in additional affluent seasonal markets.

## 6. Sensitivities & Validation

The estimates move most with three levers: ARPU (membership mix and on-demand attach), the addressable share of affluent households, and corridor density. Before using these figures externally, validate them with primary research — founding-member interviews, pilot conversion and retention, and a bottom-up count of affluent households in the target ZIP codes.
